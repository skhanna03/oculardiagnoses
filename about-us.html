Our Stories and Motivations

Founder | Developer

William Das

William is a junior at Hunter College High School in NYC. He is the co-founder of nonprofit organization Coding for Impact (codingforimpact.org) and developer behind Ocular, a revolutionary web application that bridges the socio-economic gaps pervading ADHD diagnosis, providing increased accuracy, efficiency, and accessibility. As Head of Technology at Coding for Impact, he has spearheaded the software development initiatives to aid nonprofits and charities. His inspire to develop Ocular stems from an elementary school friend whom he reconnected with, who had been diagnosed with ADHD. After hearing of how flawed the diagnosis process was, he set out to develop a tangible and applicable solution to mitigate these deficient medical practices. That's when he partnered with friend and peer Shubh Khanna to develop Ocular. The application is patent-pending and awaiting journal publication.

He interned at an artificial intelligence-based lab at NYU and worked with professors and graduate students on a fundamental 3D semantic image segmentation problem, optimizing previous methods. His research has been published alongside global science researchers and accepted by the International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI), awaiting journal publication.

Founder | Developer

Shubh Khanna

In June of 2019, my mom revealed to me that my cousin had been diagnosed with ADHD by doctors in India, and was to receive medicine to help combat his recent depression and severe mood swings.

Looking up ‘ADHD’ that night on the Internet, fervently trying to understand how it impacted the body and a person’s future, I found one statistic that stays with me today: in the U.S. alone, more than a million people are misdiagnosed with attention-deficiency/hyper-activity disorder.

But that is not the whole story — if 1 million children do not receive proper diagnosis in the United States, a developed and private healthcare country, what would the case be in rural Africa, in India, where disorders like ADHD are viewed apprehensively by traditional societies, and barely any kid is accurately diagnosed? It’s not that Indian or African kids don’t have ADHD — it’s that our society instills values and instills expensive testing that only allows a select percentage — the elite — to be appropriately diagnosed and have a better life, while the vast majority — the poor — are perpetually pushed into poverty and subjugation because of their inability to evaluate and diagnose such a common disorder.

This was the foundation behind my development of Ocular, a patent-pending, accessible platform that uses proprietary deep learning technologies to diagnose ADHD.
